Prompt 2
🧪 Method: Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR)
4
🧠 Abstract Idea (the “big picture”)

PCR is a method used to make millions of copies of a specific piece of DNA.

👉 In simple terms: it takes a tiny amount of DNA and amplifies it so scientists can study it.

🔬 Concrete Example (real-life use)

Let’s say a doctor wants to check if a patient has COVID-19:

A sample is taken from the patient (like a nasal swab)
If the virus is present, there’s a tiny amount of its genetic material
PCR is used to copy that genetic material over and over
If enough copies are detected → the test is positive
🔄 How the Method Works (simplified steps)
Heat → DNA strands separate
Cool → primers attach to target DNA
Extend → new DNA copies are built

This cycle repeats ~30 times → turning a tiny signal into something detectable

💡 Why This Matters
Used in medical testing, forensics, and research
Extremely sensitive (can detect very small amounts of DNA)
🧾 One-Sentence Summary

PCR is a lab method that copies DNA, and for example, it’s how doctors can detect viruses like COVID-19 from tiny samples.
